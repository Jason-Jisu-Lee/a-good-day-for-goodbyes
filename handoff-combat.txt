THE GAME
- 1-bit minimalist survival + incremental hybrid. Town grid of tiles
  (Rebuild-like, diamond view). Real time, no turns, PC + itch.io.
- Premise: a few survivors reclaim a ruined town tile by tile.
  Endgame: clear a sealed bunker before open ground becomes
  unholdable. Time-rewind prestige framed as saying goodbyes.
- Player is NEVER labor: attention + decisions only. You assign
  survivors to tiles/actions (scout, reclaim, gather, clear); work
  runs automatically. Survivors have names, faces, permadeath
  (under review).
- LOCKED identity: pushing into a dangerous tile = encounter where
  the player LOSES control and survivors act autonomously (some
  flee, some rush in, dramatic saves, real losses). Therefore:
  FIGHTS ARE THE EXAM, PREPARATION IS THE GAME. The player preps
  (weapons, crew choice, timing) and watches; never executes combat.
- Design principles: important fun decisions only; depth reveals
  gradually; no early punishment; concepts introduced one by one;
  all player-facing numbers are integers.
- Bounded-axes doctrine: few upgrade axes, each with a natural
  ceiling; numbers stay small; +1 feels huge. MATERIALS = the one
  universal resource (food is separate, sustenance only).
- World pacing: tier 0 = 4 origin tiles (safe), tier 1 = 8 tiles
  around them (scout 12s, will contain at least 2 danger tiles with
  1 zombie each), tier 2+ = outward rings, +12s scout time per
  tier, dangerous unscouted tiles get a slight red hint.
- Weapons already decided: 2-3 basic weapons, ~15 materials each,
  huge chunky buff vs bare fists. An item lives ON a survivor. No
  inventory UI, no durability, no ammo, no armor sprawl.

CURRENT QUESTION (continue here)
- How do survivors gain the ABILITY to craft those weapons, as
  minimally as possible?
- "Find a blueprint while scouting" was proposed and rejected as
  annoying/heavy: too much busywork per unlock.
- User wants it "kind of like real life": where and how WOULD
  people craft weapons in a ruined town?
- Workshop-as-the-only-gate felt too cheap on its own. LIBRARY
  (research over time) "makes sense" to the user.
- Candidate tiles on the table: WORKSHOP (craft site), LIBRARY
  (slow safe research), HARDWARE STORE (blueprint + materials),
  POLICE STATION (finished weapon, guarded by danger), RADIO TOWER
  (reveals a far tile), SCHOOL (train a stat), NEST (danger + rich
  loot), LOCKED SHELTER (pay materials, random payout), HIDEOUT
  (recruit + personal item), COLLAPSED STORE (rubble + cache),
  WATER TOWER, WATCHTOWER, GENERATOR, CHURCH, CEMETERY.
- Also open: how central should fighting be (identity should stay
  resource/survival-centric); stat system deferred until after the
  first combat playtest.

WORKING STYLE (matters a lot)
- Brainstorms = DISTINCT options, never one blended synthesis.
- Terse explanations, cause -> effect chains, no filler, no praise.
  Honest critique only.
- Minimalism is the soul of the game: prefer removing over adding.
- Do not invent extra systems beyond what is asked.
