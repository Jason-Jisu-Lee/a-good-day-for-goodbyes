=== THE GAME (working title "A Good Day for Goodbyes", placeholder) ===

GENRE: survival + incremental hybrid. A complete game with a real ending, not
endless.

CORE FANTASY: you are never the laborer, only attention and decisions. You
gather a handful of survivors, learn who they are, keep them alive, and spend
the whole game deciding who to save.

SETTING / PREMISE:
- You start on open ground beside a tall SEALED BUNKER.
- The ground works early but is inherently exposed; escalating enemy hordes
  will eventually overrun it. It is a countdown, not a home.
- The bunker is permanently defensible once cleared. WIN CONDITION: clear the
  entire bunker and make it self-sustaining before the ground becomes
  unholdable.

SHAPE OF A RUN (acts):
1. EARLY: land with almost nothing on a tiny origin plot (~2x2). Scavenge,
   survive the first small attacks.
2. MID: expand the plot outward in a rough circle, ring by ring. Recruit
   survivors. Automate production. Reach a maintainable equilibrium against
   escalating pressure.
3. LATE: equilibrium starts to break; attacks outgrow what open ground can
   hold. One plot contains the sealed bunker.
4. LATE-LATE (separate act): before the ground falls, take the bunker section
   by section (room by room), make it self-sustaining. That is the win.

SURVIVORS (the heart of the game):
- Recruiting is a major event, not a stat bump.
- Each has a name, a short personal story, unique base stats, and a face.
- PERMADEATH: anyone can die, forever.
- Placeholder stat axes (NOT locked): GRIT / HANDS / NERVE / HEART.
- Survivors read as quietly, slightly depressed (subtle, never caricature).

PRESTIGE (time rewind):
- A story-driven time-rewinding device. Rewinding means saying goodbye to
  survivors.
- Grants permanent buffs that attach to SPECIFIC survivors. Because a buff
  lives on a survivor, a later permanent death loses it, so prestige value is
  mostly permanent but not entirely.

PRESSURE (hordes):
- Recurring attacks, progressively harder. Every attack damages survivors
  (injury / attrition).
- Missing an event costs nothing (absence of reward, never a penalty).

ENEMY (UNDECIDED, placeholder): two candidates, (1) zombies (simplest), or
(2) AI robots that turned on humans. Not locked.

TONE / ART / AUDIO:
- Deliberate CONTRAST is a pillar: classy, understated JAZZ as background
  music, against playful, minimal 1-bit pixel art. The mismatch is the point.
- Art: extremely minimal, monochrome-classy. Small survivor bust portraits
  (dark hair, pale face, gray shoulders, two small eyes, no mouth).
- Music: jazz, already have tracks (AI-generated + royalty-free).

MY DESIGN DOCTRINE (taste to respect):
- Player is never labor: attention + decisions only; production automates the
  moment it exists.
- BOUNDED AXES: upgrade several axes each with a natural ceiling; progress =
  opening NEW axes, never inflating one number. Numbers stay small and
  watchable (never 1e47).
- Missing an event = absence of reward, never a penalty.

WHAT I WANT TO BRAINSTORM:
- The minute-to-minute and session-to-session gameplay LOOP, and how ground
  expansion, resource production, recruiting, defense/combat, and the bunker
  act connect.
- Distinct options for the open questions: enemy identity, the stat system,
  how the bunker act actually plays, and how prestige should feel.
- Anything underspecified or risky.
